OUR IMPACT

We bridge the urban-rural divide.
Rural children get equal opportunity for a better future.
Over 4500 rural kids keep pace with Technology and learn quality English.
Rural communities empowered through training and employment of women



Our Model

Sign MOUs with Block Education Officers
Adopt rural Government Primary schools
Identify educated / semi-educated homemakers from the same communities to train and employ them as Coordinators to teach English & Computers to the children of these schools
Bring resources like Laptops, Tablets and Libraries
Rural Coordinators receive weekly in-house training
Rural Coordinators also undergo training in other Institutions
Volunteers from the city visit the schools once a week to interact with the kids and the Coordinators
Kids & Coordinators are assessed regularly to gauge the impact of OBLF intervention.

Our Trustees

Anamika Majumder

Founder and Managing Trustee

Anamika is a Software Engineer and has several years of experience working in companies like Sun Microsystems and Omgeo LLC (a subsidiary of DTCC) in Massachusetts, USA.She has an under-graduate degree in Engineering from the Malaviya National Institute of Technology, (f.k.a REC) Jaipur, Rajasthan, India and a Master's in Computer Science from the University of Massachusetts, Boston. Anamika moved back to India after 12 years in May 2010 to set up One Billion Literates Foundation because she believes the need of the hour is to improve state run schools. Anamika works as a software professional in the Boston area.
